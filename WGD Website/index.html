Main Dashboard

Shows the overall demographic of biographies in Wikipedia over time.

Subclasses

Describes the category of a biography (i.e Barack Obama - AmericanLeader). The subclasses are determined by Wikipedia and are linked to each biography. Each subclasses can be used as a filter and be clicked to explore the demographics of the subclass

Gender Distribution

Initially shows the whole gender distibution of biographies in Wikipedia. It will change depending on the year clicked and subclasses applied. Specific gender can be explored by cliking on their proportion in the pie chart or their captions in the pie chart legend.

Average Age

Initally shows the average age of all the biographies. When some other filters such as the year, subclass and/or gender are clicked, it shows the average age of the biographies categorized by the filters.

Other Genders

Shows the demographic of biographies having gender other than male and female in Wikipedia over time.

About

Our project, the Wikipedia Gender Dashboard, is committed to promoting gender equality in one of the most widely used sources of information globally—Wikipedia. By visualizing data on gender representation in Wikipedia articles, we strive to create a tool that helps editors identify gaps and work towards balanced content. We believe that equitable representation of all genders is crucial to a fair and unbiased knowledge base, and we aim to support the global community in achieving this goal.

  • Data Collection

    In this work, instances of 'Person' in Wikipedia were collected, including their subclasses, gender, age, birth year, publication year, and Wikidata IDs, from 2001 to 2024. DBpedia and Wikidata endpoints were used to collect the data where most of the instance's attributes are collected via DBpedia, while gender and publication year were sourced from Wikidata. Amazon SageMaker were used for persistent data collection over 100 hours using Python on Jupyter Notebook, ensuring long-running tasks and better integration with GitHub.

  • Data Preprocessing

    To prepare the dataset for analysis, a series of steps were taken to address duplicates, incorrect values, and missing data. The dataset, comprising 979,648 rows and seven attributes (subclass, instance, wikiDataID, gender, age, birth year, and publication year), initially had 134,353 duplicate rows that were removed. Incorrect values in the age and gender columns were addressed by setting implausible ages (e.g., ages below 0 or above 100) to null and cleaning gender data with 24 unique values remaining. Missing values in gender (47,838 rows) and publication year (2,642 rows) were removed, while missing age data was retained for its demographic value. This cleaning process ensured the dataset's readiness for in-depth analysis.

  • Data Analysis & Dashboard Creation &

    The Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) visualization techniques - Univariate and Bivariate EDA were used to find the best visualizations for the the the Wikipedia Gender Dashboard before being developed into two dashboards using Microsoft Power BI. The main dashboard features a histogram showing the number of instances (articles) published over the years, a subclass filter, a pie chart of gender distribution, article counts, and average age. The other genders dashboard presents similar information but focuses on non-binary genders, which are harder to analyze in the first dashboard dominated by male and female data. These dashboards facilitate deeper analysis of gender representation on Wikipedia.

  • Usability Study

    A usability study was conducted on 15 participants—eight males and seven females, ages 19 to 29—all of whom had experience reading Wikipedia articles. Participants were given a set of tasks to evaluate and provide feedback on the dashboard, which was analyzed using thematic analysis. Feedback given by participants after encountering some issues while interacting with the dashboard was considered during the iterative process of building the dashboard. With this, we hope to create a user-friendly dashboard that everyone can use.
